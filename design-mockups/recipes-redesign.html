Healthy Big Mac Bowl
Inspired by McDonald's Big Mac
A deconstructed Big Mac in bowl form — seasoned beef, crispy lettuce, pickles, and that iconic special sauce, minus the bun.
25 min Total
520 cal Calories
2 Servings
Easy Difficulty
Ingredients
6 of 10
In Your Kitchen · 6 items
Ground beef (93% lean) 1 lb
Iceberg lettuce 1 head
White onion 1 medium
Yellow mustard 1 tbsp
Garlic powder 1 tsp
Salt & pepper to taste
Need to Buy · 4 items
Dill pickles 4 slices
American cheese 2 slices
Sesame seeds 1 tsp
Special sauce (or Thousand Island) 3 tbsp
Nutrition per Serving
42g
Protein
28g
Carbs
24g
Fat
4g
Fiber
Instructions
1
Season the ground beef with garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Form into two thin patties and cook in a hot skillet over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes per side until well-browned and cooked through.
8 min
2
While the beef cooks, shred the iceberg lettuce and dice the onion. Slice the pickles into thin rounds.
Pro tip: Ice-cold lettuce gives the best crunch
3
In the last minute of cooking, lay a slice of American cheese on each patty and cover the pan briefly to melt.
1 min
4
Build your bowls: layer shredded lettuce at the base, top with the cheeseburger patty (crumbled or whole), diced onion, and pickle slices.
5
Drizzle generously with special sauce, sprinkle sesame seeds on top, and serve immediately. Mix before eating for the full Big Mac experience!
Make it a wrap: use a low-carb tortilla instead of a bowl
